01 — Context

Online fashion has a waste problem.

About 30% of online fashion purchases are returned, generating millions of tons of waste a year — largely because shoppers can’t tell how something will actually look or fit. The gap between the online and physical experience is the root cause.

02 — The move

Remove the gamble before checkout.

Triangulated research (online ethnography, a 20-person questionnaire, and interviews) pointed to one root cause: uncertainty about fit and compatibility. The bet: an AI-powered fitting-room visualiser built on a customizable virtual self, so shoppers see compatibility before buying — and lean less on wasteful bulk-buy-and-return “bracketing.”

Heuristic evaluation and think-aloud testing then sharpened it: clearer system status (what’s being tried, total price), more explicit icons, and error prevention so users never lose their way back to the bag.

03 — Outcome

One Shop — confidence before checkout.

The final mobile-first design uses an AI fitting-room visualiser so shoppers grasp product compatibility and buy with confidence, directly targeting the return-driven waste cycle.

Next steps: develop system functionality and validate the technical feasibility of the core AI — the supporting technology isn’t yet commercial, so cost-effectiveness remains to be tested.
